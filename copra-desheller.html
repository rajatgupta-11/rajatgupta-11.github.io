Pneumatic Copra Desheller

Problem Context

In rural South India, copra (dried coconut) is manually deshelled — a labor-intensive and time-consuming process. Industrial machines exist but are unaffordable for small-scale farmers. There’s a clear need for a safer, cheaper, modular solution.

Project Objectives

  • Low-cost, lightweight, modular design
  • Use locally available components
  • Minimize user training
  • Automate with simple sensors and pneumatics

System Overview

Sensor: IR proximity sensor detects copra placement

Controller: Arduino Nano triggers air piston

Actuator: Pneumatic piston cracks the shell

Frame: Welded steel/aluminum with fixed shell cavity

Power: 12V battery-powered compressor

Key Engineering Decisions

  • Chose pneumatics for controllability and low maintenance
  • IR sensors provided non-contact, reliable detection
  • Arduino allowed flexible tuning of timing and logic
  • Shell cavity design protected the coconut meat from damage

Results (Estimated)

  • Estimated time reduction: 35s → 10s per unit
  • Cost: under ₹5,000 (~$60 USD)
  • Repairable with local tools and materials
  • Safety delay reduces risk of accidental actuation

Future Improvements

  • Add force sensors for pressure feedback
  • Introduce foot-pedal override trigger
  • Optimize for different shell sizes and conditions
  • Document and open-source the build for NGOs
